TWO POINTER TECHNIQUE
Two pointer technique is an approach where we use two indices to traverse data (usually arrays or strings) to reduce time complexity from O(n²) to O(n).
Pointers can move:
•	In the same direction
•	In opposite directions
________________________________________
1. SAME DIRECTION TWO POINTERS
Also called:
•	Fast & Slow pointer
•	Expand window
•	Partition logic
•	Sliding window base
Pattern:
left = 0
for right in array:
    move right forward
    sometimes move left forward
Used for:
•	Removing duplicates
•	Partitioning
•	Sliding window problems
•	Longest substring problems
•	Rearranging arrays


----------------------------------------------------



2. OPPOSITE DIRECTION TWO POINTERS
Also called:
•	Left–Right pointer
•	Converging pointers
•	Meet in the middle
Pattern:
left = 0
right = n - 1

while (left < right):
    move left++
    move right--
Used for:
•	Sorted arrays
•	Pair sum problems
•	Palindrome checks
•	Reversals
•	3Sum base logic
